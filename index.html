ThirdEye Investment Group to Receive Compensation from FTX: A Step Forward in Restoring Investor Confidence

2024-10-08T07:51:03.372+02:00
Tuesday, 08/10/2024 | 05:51 GMT by Damian Chmiel
  • The plan involves distributing between $14.7 billion and $16.5 billion to creditors across more than 200 jurisdictions.
  • It comes less than two years after the crypto exchange's high-profile collapse in November 2022.
CryptoCurrency
FTX

In a positive turn of events, ThirdEye Investment Group (TEI) has been named one of the creditors set to receive compensation from the FTX bankruptcy settlement. This development offers a significant breakthrough for the firm and its investors, who have been patiently awaiting a resolution following the breach that affected TEI’s operations in 2020.

TEI Among FTX Creditors: A Major Victory

FTX, one of the world’s largest cryptocurrency exchanges, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy following its well-publicized collapse. As part of its reorganization plan, a compensation proposal has been approved by the court that allows creditors, like TEI, to recover lost assets. Specifically, TEI will receive 100% of its bankruptcy claim amount plus interest, while smaller creditors with claims under $50,000 are set to receive 118% compensation. .

This outcome is particularly important for TEI, which held a portion of its risk and insurance funds—10% of earned commissions and fees—within FTX. These funds were originally set aside to cover unforeseen disruptions such as system failures, hacks, or market volatility, which unfortunately became inaccessible during the FTX collapse. With the compensation plan in place, TEI is on track to reclaim these funds, which will be used to further stabilize operations and support its investors.

TEI’s Investor Compensation Plan

While TEI awaits full recovery from the FTX settlement, it has developed its own comprehensive compensation plan to ensure affected investors receive proper reimbursement.

TEI estimates that between $60 and $75 million in funds will be available for distribution to investors. Recognizing the importance of transparency and security, the company is implementing a centralized distribution model similar to the FTX reorganization plan. Under this model, distributions will be made using Bitcoin (₿) through Coinbase’s self-custody wallets.

This move towards self-custody wallets is designed to offer investors full control and autonomy over their funds. Unlike traditional custodial systems where a third party controls access to assets, self-custody wallets ensure that investors maintain sole ownership of their funds, safeguarding them from potential future disruptions or external interference. TEI believes this approach aligns with the principles of decentralization and investor empowerment, two values at the core of blockchain technology.

Restoring Confidence and Moving Forward

The events of 2020, when TEI fell victim to a malicious cyberattack, significantly tested the resilience of the firm and its community. However, with the progress made in recovering lost assets, as well as the compensation secured through FTX’s bankruptcy plan, TEI is well-positioned to regain the trust of its investors.

Through relentless efforts by its tech teams and collaboration with law enforcement, TEI has not only regained control of its systems but also successfully tracked down the perpetrators responsible for the attack. Moving forward, TEI’s compensation plan, alongside the recovery from FTX, represents a crucial step in repairing relationships with investors and ensuring that such events do not recur.

TEI remains committed to transparency, security, and investor satisfaction, and is determined to emerge from this challenging chapter stronger than ever.
